Reflections of a Not-Quite-First-Rate Writer

Every once in a while –really every other day– I'm afflicted with a suddenly entrenched and blistering feeling of incompetence of the literary variety. Frustration boils over to a point of intellectual dormancy, and my mind is rendered out of sorts.

In my mind's eye there is a writer, reader and perpetual student less indicisive, insecure and scatterbrained than I. She remains unyieldingly tunnel focussed, intellectually robust and experienced, yet contrasts the routinized humdrum of my life with an existence so sweetly adorned with a sort of musical quality it's almost tragic; tuned perfectly, beautifully, to something akin to a Bill Evans piece. Remaining for the most part in the recesses of my mind, she silently emerges in full form at these most sordid moments of painfully abject insecurity, reminding me of all my rough edges, my flimsiness and inexperience. So, in a fit of irrationality bordering on the imbecilic, I allow my despondency –and what I consider to be her to undeniable supremacy over me– to imperceptibly stutter (but nonetheless disrupt) my progression.

I regard my skepticism to be in some ways a weakness of my being. It's not so much that I am an harshly judgemental person as I am an overly critical one; subject to much of this critique being those positions and modalities that can best be described as status quo.
